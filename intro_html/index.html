The Big Three

Who is in The Big Three of anime?

It has been a debate for years on who belongs in The Big Three. What is The Big Three you might ask? The Big Three is a term used to describe the three most popular and influential anime series that dominated the early 2000s. These series were all published in Weekley Shonen Jump and are credited with bringing anime into the global mainstream.

  • Bleach
  • One Piece
  • Naruto
  • Dragon Ball Z

      • Why are four great anime listed?

      The title for The Big Three wasn't just about quality it was about market dominance. During the mid-2000s, these three series consistently held the top spots in Shonen Jump circulation and TV ratings.

      Sadly, one must go.

      While Dragon Ball Z is arguably more famous worldwide, it isn't part of the Big Three for one specific reasons. The term The Big Three was coined to describe the three series airing the mid 2000s. Dragon Ball Z ended it's original run in 1995.
